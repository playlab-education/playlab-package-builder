Claude Cowork Skills

These are the Claude Cowork skills available to Playlab learning partners. Each skill is a slash command you can type in Claude Cowork tasks to automate a workflow. Skills can also be used without a slash command — Claude will use them based on the context of a prompt.

Skills are organized by how your day flows — from planning your day, to meeting prep, to follow-up, to communication. Remember: you can run multiple Cowork tasks simultaneously. Learn more.

To use Claude Cowork, you’ll need to download the desktop app at claude.com/download and toggle from “Chat” to “Cowork” at the top of the app.

Planning & Big Picture

Start your day, end your week, stay on top of what matters

/daily-briefing Morning Executive Briefing

Generate a morning executive briefing — a concise, prioritized snapshot of your day. Pulls calendar, email, and messaging data to surface what matters before you start.

Try it: /daily-briefing Give me my morning briefing Briefing for tomorrow What does my day look like Monday?
/weekly-recap End-of-Week Summary

Generate a concise end-of-week summary: meetings held, key emails sent, action items still open, and threads that have gone cold. Helps with Friday wrap-up or Monday planning.

Try it: /weekly-recap Give me my weekly recap Recap last week What happened this week?
🔍

Meeting Preparation

Show up informed and ready for every conversation

/meeting-prep-briefing Pre-Meeting Briefing

Generate a pre-meeting briefing for an upcoming call or meeting. Pulls context from calendar and email history to give you a clear picture before you walk in — attendee info, relationship history, open items, and talking points.

Try it: /meeting-prep-briefing Prep my next meeting Prep Sarah Chen from Acme Meeting prep for tomorrow
/meeting-scheduler Calendar Availability & Scheduling

Check your calendar availability and draft a scheduling email — with context pulled from prior conversations so the invite isn’t cold. Handles new vs. existing contacts differently.

Try it: /meeting-scheduler Schedule 30 min with Sarah next week Find time for a call with the Acme team Book a meeting with James — pilot kickoff
🤝

After Meetings

Nothing falls through the cracks after a conversation

/meeting-follow-up-email Follow-Up Email Drafter

Draft follow-up emails after meetings. Pulls recent calendar events, enriches with context, then drafts a follow-up in your voice. Shows status and recommendations (needed / simple touch / already sent / skip).

Try it: /meeting-follow-up-email Follow up on my last meeting Draft follow-ups for this week Follow up with Sarah about next steps
/meeting-action-items Action Item Extractor

Pull today’s meetings, extract all action items assigned to you, and present a clean summary. Gathers from meeting notes, sent emails, and calendar descriptions. Best run at end of day.

Try it: /meeting-action-items What are my action items from today? Pull action items from today’s meetings What did I commit to today?
/granola-hubspot-sync Meeting Notes to CRM

Sync your Granola meeting notes to HubSpot as properly formatted meeting engagements with correct date/time, attendee associations, and company links. Prevents duplicates.

Try it: /granola-hubspot-sync Sync today’s meetings to HubSpot Sync Granola notes from yesterday Sync this week’s meetings
/crm-update CRM Note Generator

After a meeting or email exchange, generate a structured CRM-ready note summarizing what happened, decisions made, and next steps. Works with any CRM — outputs formatted text or sends to a CRM forwarding address.

Try it: /crm-update CRM update for my call with Sarah today CRM update for my thread with Acme Log my last meeting to HubSpot

Discount Policy

Strategic Principle: Protect Software, Flex on Services
  • First lever: Reduce services pricing (facilitation, coaching, tool builds)
  • Last lever: Reduce software/license pricing
  • Never: Discount software to close a services-only deal
Software tier pricing is already volume-discounted by design. The tiers ARE the discount — don’t layer additional reductions.
Core Principles:
  • Always show Standard Rate alongside the partner price
  • Name every discount — use the category names below
  • “Included” not “$0” — bundled services preserve perceived value
  • Services and software discount separately
  • Hard floor: 70% of Standard Rate. Nothing below this.
  • Discounts don’t stack. Use whichever is most favorable to the partner
  • Proposal pricing valid for 60 days
  • All discounts should be run through Nate before committing to the partner

Grant-Funded Work — No Discounts. The grant is the subsidy. Price at Standard Rate. If the budget can’t cover it, reduce scope — don’t discount the rate.
When NOT to Discount:
  • Partner is grant-funded (the grant IS the subsidy)
  • Partner is asking for software discount on a services-heavy deal
  • Multiple discount categories would need to stack (pick one)
  • Partner needs below-floor pricing AND high-touch support
  • Partner wants perpetual pilot pricing beyond the first contract
In all cases: reduce scope before reducing rate.
Approval: Any discount = Run through Nate · Software discounts (except Equity) = Nate only · 15%+ = Nate only (with HubSpot justification)
