Research & Preparation

Show up informed and ready for every partner interaction

/research Organization Research Brief

Generates a comprehensive research brief on a school, district, or organization in preparation for a discovery call. Runs targeted web searches, checks internal systems for relationship history, and produces a structured brief covering demographics, leadership, strategic priorities, tech landscape, and Playlab fit.

Try it: /research Houston ISD /research Teach For America
/prep Meeting Prep Briefing

Generates a pre-meeting briefing for an upcoming call. Pulls context from calendar, past meetings, email history, CRM, and Slack. Includes relationship history, open commitments, talking points, and a tailored demo app idea.

Try it: /prep next /prep tomorrow /prep Sarah Chen
/briefing Daily Morning Briefing

Executive morning briefing that gathers meeting prep for every call that day, inbox triage, tasks, action items from yesterday, stale emails, and missed debriefs. Produces a comprehensive prioritized snapshot of the day.

Try it: /briefing
/calendly Scheduling Overview

Shows upcoming Calendly bookings, event types, and invitee details. Supports filtering by time range and name/email search.

Try it: /calendly /calendly today /calendly this week

Meeting Support & Follow-Up

Nothing falls through the cracks after a conversation

/debrief Post-Meeting Debrief

The all-in-one post-meeting command. Pulls meeting notes, email context, and CRM records. Extracts summary, decisions, and commitments. Drafts follow-up email, creates tasks, updates CRM, and prepares team notifications — all confirmed before sending.

Try it: /debrief /debrief last /debrief today /debrief Sarah Chen
/followup Meeting Follow-Up Email

Drafts a follow-up email after a meeting. Pulls recent calendar events, enriches each with email and CRM context, then presents a prioritized list. You pick one and it drafts the email.

Try it: /followup /followup last /followup this week
/meeting-actions Action Item Extractor

End-of-day action item extractor. Pulls today’s meetings and emails, extracts all action items, deduplicates against existing tasks, and adds new items to the task database.

Try it: /meeting-actions
/noshow No-Show Nudge Email

Sends a quick nudge email when someone hasn’t shown up to a meeting. Checks for recent communication first, then drafts a short warm-but-urgent email with the meeting link and reschedule option.

Try it: /noshow /noshow Sarah
/granola-sync Meeting Notes to CRM

Syncs recent meeting recordings to HubSpot as meeting engagements. Deduplicates against existing records and creates entries with correct associations and timestamps.

Try it: /granola-sync /granola-sync last 3 days /granola-sync today

Proposals, Pricing & Partnerships

From package recommendation to signed deal

/package-rec Package Recommender

Analyzes partner context (meeting notes, CRM data, emails) to recommend the best-fit Playlab package. Loads current pricing, synthesizes a partner profile, recommends pathways and tiers, and estimates pricing with line items.

Try it: /package-rec Houston ISD /package-rec [paste meeting notes]
/partnership Proposal Generator

Generates or updates a Playlab partnership proposal as a native Google Doc. Researches the org, builds SOW sections, generates pricing tables, and fills the template. Handles non-English proposals and multi-currency.

Try it: /partnership Houston ISD /partnership --update [doc URL] reduce to Core
/partnereval Opportunity Evaluator

Evaluates a partnership or grant opportunity using the REDE framework (Reach, Ease, Depth, Alignment). Scores each dimension and delivers a go/no-go decision with next steps.

Try it: /partnereval [paste email or RFP]
/rfp RFP & Grant Evaluator

Evaluates and drafts responses to RFPs, grants, or conference proposals. Three-phase process with a go/no-go decision point. Saves progress for later and generates formatted Google Docs.

Try it: /rfp [paste RFP text] /rfp [conference name or URL]
/opp Create CRM Opportunity

Creates or updates a HubSpot deal from meeting notes, a recording, or an email thread. Extracts deal info, checks for duplicates, and creates with full associations.

Try it: /opp last meeting /opp email from Sarah /opp [paste notes]
/slack-opp Slack to CRM Opportunity

Creates or updates a HubSpot deal from a document shared in a Slack channel. Reads the message, extracts linked documents, and creates the opportunity.

Try it: /slack-opp #partnerships /slack-opp [Slack message URL]

Discount Policy

Strategic Principle: Protect Software, Flex on Services
  • First lever: Reduce services pricing (facilitation, coaching, tool builds)
  • Last lever: Reduce software/license pricing
  • Never: Discount software to close a services-only deal
Software tier pricing is already volume-discounted by design. The tiers ARE the discount — don’t layer additional reductions.
Core Principles:
  • Always show Standard Rate alongside the partner price
  • Name every discount — use the category names below
  • “Included” not “$0” — bundled services preserve perceived value
  • Services and software discount separately
  • Hard floor: 70% of Standard Rate. Nothing below this.
  • Discounts don’t stack. Use whichever is most favorable to the partner
  • Proposal pricing valid for 60 days
  • All discounts should be run through Nate before committing to the partner

Grant-Funded Work — No Discounts. The grant is the subsidy. Price at Standard Rate. If the budget can’t cover it, reduce scope — don’t discount the rate.
When NOT to Discount:
  • Partner is grant-funded (the grant IS the subsidy)
  • Partner is asking for software discount on a services-heavy deal
  • Multiple discount categories would need to stack (pick one)
  • Partner needs below-floor pricing AND high-touch support
  • Partner wants perpetual pilot pricing beyond the first contract
In all cases: reduce scope before reducing rate.
Approval: Any discount = Run through Nate · Software discounts (except Equity) = Nate only · 15%+ = Nate only (with HubSpot justification)
