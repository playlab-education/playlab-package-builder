Claude

Claude Cowork is for everyone — it lets you delegate tasks to Claude using connected tools like Gmail, Calendar, and Drive. Claude Code is the developer-facing terminal tool that powers Cowork under the hood. Same engine, different interfaces. Most of you will use Cowork; the Power Users tab includes Claude Code examples to show what’s possible at the edges.

Skills are slash commands you can type in Cowork tasks, or Claude will use them automatically based on context. You can run multiple tasks simultaneously. Learn more.

To get started, download the desktop app at claude.com/download and toggle from “Chat” to “Cowork” at the top of the app.

Claude Cowork Skills for Playlab

These are the Cowork skills built specifically for the Playlab team. Each skill is a slash command you can type in a Cowork task — or Claude will use them automatically based on context. Skills are organized by how your day flows.

Planning & Big Picture

Start your day, end your week, stay on top of what matters

/daily-briefing Morning Executive Briefing

Generate a morning executive briefing — a concise, prioritized snapshot of your day. Pulls calendar, email, and messaging data to surface what matters before you start.

Try it: /daily-briefing Give me my morning briefing Briefing for tomorrow What does my day look like Monday?
/weekly-recap End-of-Week Summary

Generate a concise end-of-week summary: meetings held, key emails sent, action items still open, and threads that have gone cold. Helps with Friday wrap-up or Monday planning.

Try it: /weekly-recap Give me my weekly recap Recap last week What happened this week?

Meeting Preparation

Show up informed and ready for every conversation

/meeting-prep-briefing Pre-Meeting Briefing

Generate a pre-meeting briefing for an upcoming call or meeting. Pulls context from calendar and email history to give you a clear picture before you walk in — attendee info, relationship history, open items, and talking points.

Try it: /meeting-prep-briefing Prep my next meeting Prep Sarah Chen from Acme Meeting prep for tomorrow
/meeting-scheduler Calendar Availability & Scheduling

Check your calendar availability and draft a scheduling email — with context pulled from prior conversations so the invite isn’t cold. Handles new vs. existing contacts differently.

Try it: /meeting-scheduler Schedule 30 min with Sarah next week Find time for a call with the Acme team Book a meeting with James — pilot kickoff

After Meetings

Nothing falls through the cracks after a conversation

/meeting-follow-up-email Follow-Up Email Drafter

Draft follow-up emails after meetings. Pulls recent calendar events, enriches with context, then drafts a follow-up in your voice. Shows status and recommendations (needed / simple touch / already sent / skip).

Try it: /meeting-follow-up-email Follow up on my last meeting Draft follow-ups for this week Follow up with Sarah about next steps
/meeting-action-items Action Item Extractor

Pull today’s meetings, extract all action items assigned to you, and present a clean summary. Gathers from meeting notes, sent emails, and calendar descriptions. Best run at end of day.

Try it: /meeting-action-items What are my action items from today? Pull action items from today’s meetings What did I commit to today?
/granola-hubspot-sync Meeting Notes to CRM

Sync your Granola meeting notes to HubSpot as properly formatted meeting engagements with correct date/time, attendee associations, and company links. Prevents duplicates.

Try it: /granola-hubspot-sync Sync today’s meetings to HubSpot Sync Granola notes from yesterday Sync this week’s meetings
/crm-update CRM Note Generator

After a meeting or email exchange, generate a structured CRM-ready note summarizing what happened, decisions made, and next steps. Works with any CRM — outputs formatted text or sends to a CRM forwarding address.

Try it: /crm-update CRM update for my call with Sarah today CRM update for my thread with Acme Log my last meeting to HubSpot

Context & Instructions

Give Cowork persistent context so it works the way you want every time

Global Instructions
In the Claude Desktop app, go to Settings > Cowork and edit “Global Instructions.” This is your always-on context: your role, preferred tone, output formats, and tools you use. Applies to every Cowork session automatically.
Project Instructions
Each Cowork Project has its own instructions field. Use this for workstream-specific context (e.g., “This project handles partnership proposals. Always use Playlab brand voice and reference our pricing tiers.”).
Context Files
Create markdown files in your working folder that Cowork reads automatically: about-me.md (your role and priorities), voice-and-style.md (tone and formatting), working-rules.md (behavior preferences). This is the Cowork equivalent of CLAUDE.md in Claude Code.

Keeping HubSpot Accurate

We’re all responsible for keeping HubSpot up to date so it reflects reality — pipeline stage, deal owner, amounts, contacts, and engagement notes. If something has changed with your partner, it should change in HubSpot.

Two automations touch HubSpot: the proposal bot (when you run it) and a nightly sync that refreshes deal fields from the proposal doc linked on the opportunity. Even so, focus on manually updating when possible — automations are a safety net, not the system of record.

Outreach — Where Yours Comes From
SourceWhat to Do
Target-list prospectingWork your ICP list — districts, networks, and orgs that fit Playlab’s sweet spot. Research, personalize, reach out.
Conference follow-throughEvery booth conversation, session connection, or badge scan is a warm lead. Follow up within a week while it’s fresh.
Referral activationYour existing partners are your best source. Ask for intros to peers, colleagues, and adjacent orgs.

Outreach Workflow
  1. 1Pick your targets — pull your ICP list, review conferences you’ve attended, scan your warm network
  2. 2Research before reaching out — run /research [org name] in Claude Cowork, check HubSpot for prior touches
  3. 3Personalize the first touch — reference something real. Use /email-draft as a starting point, not a template send
  4. 4Log it in HubSpot immediately — create the deal at “Discovery” with yourself as owner. Use /opp.
  5. 5Run a real cadence — 3–5 touches over 2–3 weeks across channels (email + LinkedIn)
  6. 6Goal of every touch: book a discovery conversation. Don’t pitch, don’t quote, don’t demo — earn the call.
✏️
Manual: For Outreach, create the HubSpot deal before your first touch. For Inquiry, confirm the auto-created deal looks right after Nate’s routing.
✅ Best Practices
  • Inquiry assigned to you? Respond within 24 hours — Nate’s warmth is your runway, don’t waste it
  • Personalize every first touch — reference something real and specific
  • Run a cadence (3–5 touches, multi-channel) for outreach — not a single email
  • Create the HubSpot deal before the first outreach touch, not after
  • Don’t pitch in the first email — earn the discovery call first
  • Track what’s working — which lists, events, referral sources convert? Share in #partnerships

Deep Dive

— disco/demo flow, questions, SPICED, listening, objections, renewals ▼
🔄
Renewals (on hold): For renewals, discovery is a check-in, not a first conversation. Focus on: What went well? What do they want more or less of? Budget changed? New priorities? You can often skip the ACE opener and demo — go straight to the relationship.
Ask questions first, demo second. A demo without context is a pitch, not a conversation.
Part 1 Disco — Learn about the organization
Before the Call
  1. 1Research the org and person — use Claude Cowork /meeting-prep-briefing or check HubSpot, email, their website, and recent news
On the Call: ACE Opener (5 min)
Appreciate (“Thanks for your time”) → Check time (“We have 30 min, still work?”) → End goal (“I’d love to learn about your priorities and see if Playlab could help”)
On the Call: Ask Questions (20 min)
The partner should be talking 60%+ of this time. One question at a time, then listen.
QuestionWhat You’re Learning
“Tell me about where you all are on your AI journey?”Current state — tools, policies, adoption level
“What’s working and what’s not with what you’re doing now?”Pain points and gaps
“What would make this a win for your team in 6 months?”Success criteria
“How quickly are you looking to move? Next year, or this spring?”Timeline, urgency, budget cycle
“Who else should be at the next meeting?”Decision-makers, stakeholders
Part 2 Demo — Share Playlab with them
Before the Call
  1. 1Prep 2–3 example Playlab apps relevant to their context (from Playlab Gardens)
  2. 2Have an app idea ready to build live that relates to their role — the live build is your strongest demo
On the Call: Run the Demo (10 min)
Ask permission: “Would it help to see Playlab in action?” Then:
  1. 1Build live — start from a blank app and build something tied to what they just said in disco
  2. 2Walk through 1–2 examples from Playlab Gardens that match their context
  3. 3Tie it back — “This is the kind of thing [their educators / their leaders] could build in 20 minutes.”
🎯
Ideally they see both a live build and an example app. The build shows how easy it is; the examples show what’s possible.
On the Call: Close (5 min)
Confirm next steps. “What questions do you have? What’s resonating? Who else should be in the next conversation?” Schedule the follow-up before the call ends.
After the Call
  1. 1Run /debrief in Claude Cowork — it summarizes the call, drafts a follow-up email, and updates HubSpot
  2. 2Send follow-up email within 24 hours — use /meeting-follow-up-email or /email-draft
  3. 3Move the deal to “Scoping” yourself when ready to scope a package
⚙️
Automated: Run /debrief after the call to draft a follow-up email, log the meeting in HubSpot, and create Notion action items — you review before anything sends. Run /granola-hubspot-sync weekly.
✏️
Manual: Deal stays at “Discovery” until you move it to “Scoping” yourself.

Objection Handling
Feel, Felt, Found: “I understand how you feel… Others have felt this way… But what they found is…”
Confirm, Clarify, Continue: “So you’re saying [restate]… That’s actually not the case, here’s why… Does that address your concern?”
✅ Best Practices
  • Schedule the follow-up meeting before ending the call
  • Let the partner talk 60%+ of the time — ask one question, then listen
  • Understand their priorities before demoing — questions first, always
  • Confirm next steps out loud before closing the call

Pricing Rules of Thumb
  1. ✓Protect software, flex on services. First lever is reducing services (facilitation, coaching, tool builds). Last lever is discounting software — the tiers are already volume-discounted. See the Discount Policy on the Pricing Sheet for categories, floors, and approvals.
  2. ✓Always recommend first session in-person, rest virtual — price high first, offer virtual to reduce. It’s easier to lower a price than raise one.
  3. ✓Before confirming travel costs, check with Mera or Hilah on LP availability in the partner’s region. Don’t assume a local LP is available.
  4. ✓Show Standard Rate alongside any adjusted price — never hide the value of what you’re offering.
  5. ✓When in doubt, build two options and let the partner choose.
  6. ✓For Year 1 Play partners, include enrollment-based pricing as a reference for Year 2 budgeting.

🔄
Renewals (on hold): Existing partners often have lighter approval requirements — procurement already has Playlab on file, legal already reviewed the MSA. Confirm what’s actually needed before assuming they’ll skip it.

Your Playbook
  1. 1Ask early: “What does your internal approval process look like? Who needs to see this?” — ideally during Discovery, definitely by Scoping
  2. 2Make your champion’s job easy — give them the one-pager, the talking points, the budget case. They’re selling Playlab internally, not you.
  3. 3Offer to attend stakeholder meetings — sometimes a live demo seals it
  4. 4Check in weekly — short, friendly status requests. Partners appreciate momentum.
  5. 5Route procurement/legal/MSA requests to Nate — don’t negotiate terms yourself
  6. 6Once approved, confirm next steps with the partner and transition to Contract (Stage 5 — runs in parallel with Kickoff & Delivery)
✏️
Manual: Update the HubSpot deal stage to reflect where they are in their approval process — use the notes field to capture what’s blocking and who’s deciding.
✅ Best Practices
  • Ask the approval question in Discovery — knowing the gates up front saves months
  • Your champion is the hero, not you. Arm them, don’t go around them.
  • If a partner goes silent in approvals, assume procedural friction, not lost interest — send a warm status check
  • Route MSA/legal/contract redlines to Nate immediately — don’t commit to anything
  • If the process drags past 60 days, flag Nate to help unstick or assess the deal

Discount Policy

Strategic Principle: Protect Software, Flex on Services
  • First lever: Reduce services pricing (facilitation, coaching, tool builds)
  • Last lever: Reduce software/license pricing
  • Never: Discount software to close a services-only deal
Software tier pricing is already volume-discounted by design. The tiers ARE the discount — don’t layer additional reductions.
Core Principles:
  • Always show Standard Rate alongside the partner price
  • Name every discount — use the category names below
  • “Included” not “$0” — bundled services preserve perceived value
  • Services and software discount separately
  • Hard floor: 70% of Standard Rate. Nothing below this.
  • Discounts don’t stack. Use whichever is most favorable to the partner
  • Proposal pricing valid for 60 days
  • All discounts should be run through Nate before committing to the partner

When NOT to Discount:

  • Partner is asking for software discount on a services-heavy deal
  • Multiple discount categories would need to stack (pick one)
  • Partner needs below-floor pricing AND high-touch support
  • Partner wants perpetual pilot pricing beyond the first contract
In all cases: reduce scope before reducing rate.
